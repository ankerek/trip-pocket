# Trip Pocket — Marketing Strategy

**Living document · last updated 2026-05-11**

> Build a defensible travel brand on Instagram + TikTok that compounds independent of paid acquisition, and arrives at App Store launch with built-in distribution.

This is the marketing strategy for Trip Pocket. It's a long-living doc — updated as the brand evolves, not a one-shot plan. Anchor decisions to the principles here; if reality diverges, update this doc rather than drifting silently.

Sibling docs: [`PRODUCT.md`](./PRODUCT.md) (what the app is), [`ROADMAP.md`](./ROADMAP.md) (product roadmap), [`ARCHITECTURE.md`](./ARCHITECTURE.md), [`RELEASE.md`](./RELEASE.md).

---

## 1. Goal & success criteria

### North star

Establish a recognizable Trip Pocket brand presence on Instagram and TikTok that:

1. Builds a defensible audience that compounds independent of paid acquisition.
2. Outlives any single feature or pivot of the product.
3. Arrives at App Store launch with built-in distribution and warm demand.

### 12-month hard metrics

| Channel                   | Metric                    | Target                                              |
| ------------------------- | ------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Instagram                 | Followers                 | 10,000                                              |
| Instagram                 | Saves-per-post (of reach) | ≥ 10%                                               |
| TikTok                    | Followers                 | 15,000                                              |
| Pinterest (bonus channel) | Monthly impressions       | 50,000                                              |
| Brand                     | Visual recognition test   | Audience identifies posts without seeing the handle |

### What we explicitly do not optimize for

- Raw views (vanity).
- Follower count via giveaways or follow-for-follow.
- Paid ads (not in scope for this strategy — revisit post-launch).
- Comment volume from rage-bait or controversy.

**Saves and shares are the metrics that matter.** A save proves the audience genuinely wants the content. A share proves it travels. Likes and views are noise.

## 2. Positioning & voice

### Positioning line (internal compass — not a tagline)

_The travel account that helps you remember the places worth going — and gives you an app to keep them in._

### Niche

**Destination-deep: Japan + Asia.** Builds authority fast, supports strong SEO/search behavior on Pinterest and TikTok, and reflects existing expertise. Other regions are out of scope until the Asia brand is established.

### Voice traits

- **Specific, not aspirational.** Not "Tokyo is magical." Yes "this tonkatsu in Shibuya is ¥1,800 and worth the 25-min queue at 11:30am on a Tuesday." Travel audiences are saturated on vibes; they want intel.
- **Confident curator, not breathless tourist.** We've already pre-screened; the post is the recommendation.
- **Quiet about the app.** The app appears in bio, end-frame, and occasional caption CTA — never as the hook. Brand earns trust first; the product mention is the reward.
- **Geographically honest.** Tag exact neighborhoods. Link real Maps pins. Never imply you were somewhere if you weren't (especially with AI content — see §4).

### Visual signature

The brand's visual identity mirrors a Trip Pocket place card. Every video uses the same template frame at the open and close:

- Neighborhood label, small caps (e.g. `KIYOSUMI · TOKYO`).
- Place name, large display serif (e.g. _Blue Bottle Kiyosumi_).
- Category chip: `▣ Place` / `◍ Food` / `▲ Activity`.
- Cost row + best-time row.

Palette: muted travel neutrals + one accent color matching the app's brand (final palette pending app design lock).

Music: cinematic but understated. Instrumentals or ambient. **No spoken-word viral audio** — it dates the content; we want evergreen.

## 3. Brand architecture

**Decision:** hub-and-spoke, spokes deferred. Single master account through at least month 6; country accounts only when the data justifies them.

### Rationale

- **Capacity is the binding constraint.** At 3 posts/week solo while building the app, splitting across multiple accounts means lower quality everywhere — and low-quality niche content performs worse than high-quality broad content.
- **Brand consolidation pays at launch.** One megaphone with all the followers beats five fragmented audiences that are each too small to move the needle on App Store launch.
- **The product is multi-country.** A real Trip Pocket user collects Japan + Korea + Thailand in the same app. The brand should match the product surface, not split it.
- **Niche clarity inside one account is solvable.** Use TikTok playlists, IG highlights/hashtags, and Pinterest boards to create per-country browsing paths without splitting the audience.

### Hub-and-spoke plan

| Phase                        | Action                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Month 0 (now)                | Lock `@trippocket.app` as hub. Park country handles defensively (see below).                                                                                                                                           |
| Months 1–6                   | Run only the hub. Tag content by country. Use TikTok playlists / IG highlights / Pinterest boards for per-country browsing.                                                                                            |
| Month 6 data review          | Review saves/follows by country tag. A country earns a spoke only if it produces ~3–5× the engagement of the rest of the hub combined.                                                                                 |
| Month 6+ (if data justifies) | Spin up the first spoke (likely `@trippocket.japan`). Cross-promote from hub on launch day so the spoke starts with audience, not zero. Hub keeps 2/3 cadence; spoke takes the 3rd slot plus its own original content. |
| Future spokes                | Only spun up with proven data. Never speculatively.                                                                                                                                                                    |

### Defensive handle parking (Week 0)

Claim and park these handles on Instagram + TikTok now, even with no intent to use them soon. Each gets one intro post: _"Follow `@trippocket.app` — country-specific content coming soon."_

- `@trippocket.japan`
- `@trippocket.korea`
- `@trippocket.vietnam`
- `@trippocket.thailand`
- `@trippocket.indonesia`
- `@trippocket.philippines`

This costs ~30 minutes total and preserves optionality. Squatters on a brand-adjacent handle after the app launches is a permanent, unfixable annoyance.

### Anti-pattern: do not spin up speculative country accounts

If a country hasn't already proven itself within the hub data, do not launch a spoke for it. "I'm about to travel to X so I'll start `@trippocket.X`" is a trap — it fragments the brand for no audience gain.

## 4. Audience

**Primary:** millennials and Gen Z who already screenshot constantly from Instagram/TikTok and plan trips around social-media inspiration. Frequent travelers, digital nomads, and remote workers — the same audience identified in [`PRODUCT.md`](./PRODUCT.md).

**Geographic affinity:** anyone planning Japan + Asia travel — a globally massive, evergreen search demand. Especially strong from English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia) and SEA-curious Europeans.

**Mental state we target:** the moment a user has just screenshotted something travel-related and felt a flicker of "I'll never find this again." That moment is when Trip Pocket clicks. Our content reinforces that mental loop without naming it.

## 5. The three content pillars

Three posts per week. Each post fills exactly one pillar. The pillars are complementary: Pillar 1 builds brand, Pillar 2 drives reach, Pillar 3 differentiates.

### Pillar 1 — Saved Place (weekly · the flagship)

One specific place per post. Café, viewpoint, restaurant, neighborhood, hike — single subject, deep treatment.

- **Length:** 30–45 seconds.
- **Structure:**
  - 0–2s: hook frame (visual-signature card overlay).
  - 2–25s: B-roll of the place + text-overlay intel (price, hours, best time, what to order).
  - 25–35s: "why it's worth saving" line.
  - 35–45s: sign-off frame (`Save to your Tokyo trip · @trippocket.app`).
- **Hook patterns:**
  - "The café most travelers miss in Tokyo."
  - "This is on no top-10 list and I keep going back."
  - "If you save one Kyoto spot from this account, save this."
- **Repurposing:** same research becomes IG carousel (7 slides of intel) + 1–3 Pinterest pins. **One unit of research → 4 distributions.**

### Pillar 2 — Themed List (weekly · the reach play)

5–7 places under one tight theme.

- **Length:** 45–60 seconds.
- **Structure:** punchy hook (1–3s), then 5–7 rapid clips with overlay labels (5–7s each), ending with a "save all 5 to your trip" sign-off.
- **Hook patterns:**
  - "5 Tokyo neighborhoods most travelers skip."
  - "5 Kyoto spots I'd send my best friend to."
  - "7 Asian cities under $50/day in 2026."
- **Repurposing:** the 5–7 places in each list become the scout list for future Pillar 1 deep-dives. The list is also the content-pipeline funnel.

### Pillar 3 — Imagined Journey (weekly · the AI-led distinctive)

Stylized AI vignette of a place, paired with concrete real-world tips.

- **Length:** 20–30 seconds (shorter; AI footage holds attention less than real B-roll).
- **Structure:** AI cinematic sequence in a distinct illustrative style + your real-world tip overlays for that place + an explicit watermark/disclaimer corner (`illustrative · real place, real tips`).
- **Hook patterns:**
  - "Imagine your slowest morning in Hoi An …"
  - "Kyoto if you only had one autumn afternoon …"
  - "Tokyo's last shokudo at midnight …"
- **Style guardrail:** the AI must look **clearly stylized** — painterly, illustrative, dreamlike. **Never photorealistic.** Audiences accept "imagined" content; they punish "tried to fool me."

## 6. Production system

### Weekly workflow (target: 6–8 hrs total)

| Day      | Task                                                                          | Time     |
| -------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------- |
| Monday   | Pick 3 subjects, gather references, real tips, addresses, prices              | 1–1.5 hr |
| Tue–Wed  | Edit all 3 posts in one batch (templates fill-in-the-blank, not from-scratch) | 3–4 hr   |
| Thursday | Captions, build IG carousel version, build Pinterest pins                     | 1 hr     |
| Fri–Sun  | Post one per day, engage in the first hour                                    | 0.5–1 hr |

**Realism check:** 3 posts/week of faceless cinematic content is real work. If after 4 weeks the time-budget consistently spills past 10 hrs/week, drop to 2 posts/week (1× Saved Place + 1× alternating List/Imagined) rather than letting cadence become unreliable. Algorithm consistency > pillar variety.

### Toolkit

- **Editing:** CapCut or Premiere (whichever is fastest in hand).
- **Templates:** locked-in design template per pillar (After Effects or CapCut). Production becomes variation, not creation.
- **AI for Pillar 3:** pick one tool (Sora / Veo / Runway / Kling) and stay with it for visual consistency. Style brief: illustrative / painterly / dreamlike, never photorealistic.
- **AI for assist (any pillar):** caption drafting, hook brainstorming, hashtag research, alt-text generation. No disclosure needed.
- **Scheduling:** Buffer or Meta's native scheduler for IG. TikTok scheduling is unreliable — upload natively.
- **Backlog:** Notion content calendar + a `place ideas` doc (target 50 entries before posting begins).

### AI policy (non-negotiable, for trust)

- Pillar 3 AI footage is **always explicitly labeled** — `illustrative` watermark in-frame + the AI-content disclosure toggle on at upload (IG + TikTok both have this).
- Pillars 1 and 2 use **real footage only** — your own clips or licensed UGC with credit. **No AI footage of real-place specifics, ever.**
- AI-assisted _text_ (captions, hooks) is fine and needs no disclosure.
- This is a brand-safety rule, not a stylistic preference. One "you AI'd a real place and called it real" exposé sets the account back six months.

### UGC rights

- When using a creator's clip: DM for written permission, credit them in the caption, tag them in the video.
- Maintain a simple Notion log: clip, source, permission link, credit line.
- Free-license stock (Pexels, Pixabay) is acceptable but treat as last resort — audiences increasingly spot stock and disengage.

## 7. Platform behavior & app integration

### Instagram

- **Reels** are the growth engine (Pillars 1 and 3 are vertical short video — perfect fit).
- **Carousels** are the save engine — the deep "intel" version of each Saved Place. Highest save-rate format on IG in 2026.
- **Bio:** `Trip Pocket · Save the places worth going. · App for iOS [link]`
- **Pinned posts (3):** one Saved Place flagship, one List, one Imagined Journey — so a new visitor learns what the brand is in 3 seconds.
- **Stories:** rare — only behind-the-scenes (a real trip, a cut place). Keep the grid focused.

### TikTok

- Same vertical asset as Reels, but **swap the music** — TikTok's algorithm rewards native TikTok sounds. Use trending-but-evergreen instrumentals or ambient. Never spoken-word viral audio.
- **Cover frame** must work standalone — TikTok profiles are judged by cover thumbnails. Use the visual-signature card frame as the post hook _and_ the cover.
- **Captions** front-load 2–3 specific keywords (e.g. `kyoto café kiyomizu`) for in-app search.
- **Hooks are harder on TikTok** — assume 1-second judgment. Cold opens with the signature visual frame outperform "Hey guys, today I'm sharing …".

### Pinterest (bonus channel, high ROI per hour spent)

- Every Pillar 1 and Pillar 2 post becomes 1–3 Pinterest pins (different titles/descriptions, same image).
- Pinterest is the only platform where a pin from month 3 still drives traffic in month 18.
- At 3 posts/week, expect 600+ pins by month 12 — meaningful evergreen distribution.
- Pin links go to a simple landing page (`trippocket.app`) with App Store badge + email capture (landing TBD).

### App integration ladder (subtle → explicit)

Listed from most-passive to most-explicit. Default posture is restraint.

1. **Visual signature frame** — every post. The app's design language _is_ the brand. Earns passive app-equity.
2. **End-frame on every video** (1.5 sec, soft): `Save to your [trip] · trippocket.app`.
3. **Caption CTA every 3rd post:** `Save this for your Japan trip → link in bio.`
4. **Bio:** app link + one-liner (always present).
5. **Story highlights (after ~100 posts):** "How to use Trip Pocket" — actual demo, shown only to people already exploring the profile.
6. **Never:** pre-roll app pitches, breathless "tap the link" verbal CTAs, app screenshots as primary content. Audiences punish overt promo.

## 8. KPIs & decision rules

### Track weekly (one spreadsheet, one screenshot Sunday night)

Per post:

- Views
- Saves
- Shares
- Comments
- Save-rate (saves ÷ views) — **the primary metric**
- Share-rate
- Profile visits
- Follows-from-post

Per pillar (rolling 4-week):

- Which pillar drives the most saves?
- Which drives the most follows?
- Which drives the most profile visits?

Cross-platform:

- Which posts cross-pollinate (TikTok → IG follow, IG → Pinterest pin saves)?

### Decision rules

- **First 4 weeks:** do not change format even if posts flop. Algorithms need consistency to learn the account.
- **Week 8 review:** if one pillar is bottom-quartile on both saves _and_ shares, drop it. Double-down on the strongest pillar.
- **Week 12 review:** settle on the single strongest hook style and use it 70% of the time.
- **Anytime:** if a post overperforms, follow up on the same place or theme within 7 days to learn what's working.

## 9. First 30 days

### Week 0 (this week)

- Lock `@trippocket.app` handle (done 2026-05-11; `@trippocket` and `@trip.pocket` were already taken).
- **Park defensive country handles** on IG + TikTok (see §3): `@trippocket.japan`, `@trippocket.korea`, `@trippocket.vietnam`, `@trippocket.thailand`, `@trippocket.indonesia`, `@trippocket.philippines`. One intro post each pointing to the hub. ~30 min total.
- Finalize bio copy + profile photo (use the app icon at `assets/pocket-trip-icon-2.png` or final variant).
- Build the 3 pillar templates in CapCut (one per pillar, locked-in design).
- Set up Notion content calendar.
- Build the `place ideas` backlog — target 50 entries before posting.
- Set up Pinterest business account linked to landing page.

### Week 1

- Post 1× Saved Place — your strongest Tokyo example.
- Post 1× Themed List — "5 Tokyo spots that aren't on the list everyone shares."
- Post 1× Imagined Journey — first AI-style test (Kyoto autumn vignette).
- Cross-post all three to TikTok.
- Repurpose Saved Place into IG carousel + 2 Pinterest pins.

### Week 2

- Repeat full cadence.
- By end of week 2: Pinterest account has 4–6 evergreen pins live.

### Weeks 3–4

- If any post is overperforming, post a follow-up on the same place or theme to learn what's working.
- Stay disciplined on the 3-pillar mix — do not pivot yet.

### Day 30 review

- Pull metrics from all three platforms.
- Identify the highest save-rate pillar.
- Plan month 2 around what worked, but keep the 3-pillar structure intact through at least week 8.

## 10. Risks & known constraints

| Risk                                            | Mitigation                                                                                                         |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| 3 posts/week is unsustainable alongside app dev | Drop to 2/week before missing a week. Consistency > variety.                                                       |
| AI footage gets flagged or backfires            | Strict "illustrative only" rule (§6). Always-on AI disclosure. Watermark in-frame.                                 |
| Audience reads the account as promo             | "Quiet about the app" voice (§2). Restraint ladder for app mentions (§7).                                          |
| Premature country-account split                 | Hub-and-spoke discipline (§3) — no spoke without 6 months of hub data.                                             |
| One viral post sets unrealistic expectations    | First 4 weeks: don't change format. Algorithms need consistency to learn the account.                              |
| Faceless cinematic is saturated on Asia travel  | Visual-signature frame is the differentiator. No frame, no post.                                                   |
| Pillar 3 (AI) underperforms                     | Pre-committed: if bottom-quartile saves+shares at week 8, drop Pillar 3 and run 2× Saved Place + 1× List per week. |
